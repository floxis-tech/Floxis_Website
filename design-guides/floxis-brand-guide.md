# Floxis Brand Guidelines

**Version 02 — 2025**

## Brand Concept

### FLOXIS = Flow + Axis

The brand name merges two core ideas:

| Concept | Visual Expression | Meaning |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| **Flow** | Smooth, curved lines and gradients | Movement, creativity, data flow |
| **Axis** | Balanced structure and symmetry | Precision, stability, strategy |

Together, they symbolize **dynamic digital creativity built on structure** — perfect for a digital advertising or tech-forward brand.

---

## Logo

### The Abstract "F" Mark

The logo is composed of **two sweeping curves** forming an abstract "F" shape:

**Design Elements:**
- **Sense of motion** — representing flowing data or creative energy
- **Dual-layer structure** — echoing multiple digital streams or marketing channels converging at one point (the "axis")
- **Bottom curve's uplift** — suggests upward momentum and growth, key in advertising and performance-driven work

**Character:**
- Minimal yet meaningful
- Recognizable both as a letterform and a symbolic shape
- Uses cyan-to-purple gradient

### Logo Wordmark
- Font: **Futura Bold** (or similar geometric sans-serif)
- All caps: **FLOXIS**
- Paired with the abstract "F" icon

## Visual Language

### Background Treatment
- **Primary**: Dark/Black backgrounds
- **Accent**: Purple wave/flow graphics
- **Effect**: Subtle gradient glows

### Motion & Flow
The brand emphasizes flowing, wave-like visuals that suggest:
- Data in motion
- Digital transformation
- Seamless connectivity

### Grid Patterns
Subtle grid overlays can be used to represent:
- Structure and precision
- The "axis" part of the brand concept
- Technical infrastructure

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Use dark backgrounds as primary
- Apply gradient to accent elements (buttons, icons, highlights)
- Maintain generous whitespace
- Use flowing, curved visual elements
- Keep typography clean and bold

### Don't
- Use the extended palette as primary colors
- Overwhelm designs with too many gradient elements
- Use light backgrounds as primary (dark-first brand)
- Distort or modify the logo proportions
- Mix too many colors from the extended palette
