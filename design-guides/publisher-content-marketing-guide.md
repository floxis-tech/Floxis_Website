# Floxis Publisher Page: Content & Marketing Guide

## Overview
This guide outlines what data, content, and messaging to show on the Floxis publisher landing page based on analysis of Aditude and Assertive Yield—two leading publisher-focused ad tech platforms.

---

## Key Insight: What Publishers Actually Care About

From analyzing both competitors, publishers evaluate solutions based on:
1. **Control** - Will I lose control of my stack/demand?
2. **Revenue Impact** - Will this actually increase my earnings?
3. **Trust** - Is this company legitimate and experienced?
4. **Ease** - Will this complicate my operations?
5. **Risk** - What's the downside?

**Subheadline:**

| Element | Aditude | Assertive Yield | Floxis |

## Page Structure Summary (4 Viewports Max)

Keep the landing page tight and focused. Publishers are busy — don't make them scroll through redundant content.

### Recommended Structure

| Viewport | Section | Content |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **1** | **Hero** | Headline + subheadline + primary CTA + optional client logos strip |
| **2** | **Stack Diagram** | "Where Floxis Sits in Your Stack" — this IS your differentiator |
| **3** | **Value Props** | 3-4 consolidated feature cards (merged from value props + why floxis) |
| **4** | **CTA** | Final call-to-action with optional single testimonial quote above |

---

### Viewport 1: Hero

**Must include:**
- Badge: "Server-Side Infrastructure"
- Headline: "Server-Side Infrastructure for Publisher-Controlled Monetization"
- Subheadline: "Activate and monetize demand within your existing ad stack — without disrupting auctions or control."
- Primary CTA: "Request a Technical Overview"
- Secondary CTA: "See how Floxis fits your stack →"

**Optional:**
- Client logos strip (if you have recognizable names)
- Trust metric (e.g., "X+ publishers" — only if real)

---

### Viewport 2: Stack Diagram

**Purpose:** This is your trust diagram. It visually proves you don't hijack the stack.

**Must include:**
- Section title: "Where Floxis Sits in Your Stack"
- The 4-layer diagram (Demand → Floxis → Ad Server → Inventory)
- Left callout: "Your Stack. Your Demand. Your Rules."
- Right callout: "No Auction Interference"
- Connector labels: "Standard server-side integrations" / "Standards-aligned request routing"

**Do NOT:**
- Add extra complexity
- Show client-side elements
- Use marketing language here — keep it factual

---

### Viewport 3: Value Props (Consolidated)

Merge the best points from "Value Props" and "Why Floxis" into 3-4 cards:

| Card | Headline | Key Point |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **Publisher-Controlled** | Full control over stack, demand, priorities — no interference |
| 2 | **Server-Side Performance** | No client-side overhead, faster pages, better Core Web Vitals |
| 3 | **Standards-Aligned** | Built on oRTB/Prebid Server, works with existing wrappers |
| 4 | **Fast Activation** | Live in 24 hours, real human support (optional — can cut to 3 cards) |

**Layout:** 3 cards in a row (or 2x2 grid on mobile)

---

### Viewport 4: CTA Section

**Must include:**
- Headline: "Ready to see how Floxis fits your stack?"
- Subtext: Short, direct (e.g., "Test live in 24 hours.")
- Primary CTA button: "Request a Technical Overview"

**Optional:**
- Single testimonial quote above the CTA box (if you have a real one with a named person)
- If no real testimonial, skip it entirely — fake quotes destroy trust

---

### What to Cut (Move to Secondary Pages)

| Section | Why Cut | Where to Move |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| **How It Works (4 steps)** | Onboarding detail, not landing essential | Dedicated "How It Works" or "Process" page |
| **Why Floxis (3 cards)** | Redundant with Value Props | Merge best points into Value Props |
| **Testimonials (3 quotes)** | Takes a full viewport, often skimmed | Use 1 quote in CTA section OR dedicated case studies page |
| **Metrics section** | Only valuable if numbers are real and impressive | Add to Hero as small trust badges if available |

---

### Why 4 Viewports Works

1. **Respect publisher time** — They're evaluating dozens of vendors
2. **Reduce cognitive load** — Every section should earn its space
3. **Faster decision** — Hero → Diagram → Benefits → CTA is a complete story
4. **Mobile-friendly** — Fewer sections = better mobile experience
5. **Higher conversion** — Less scrolling to reach CTA

## Section 11: Animation & Motion Design (Inspired by Phantom)

Based on analysis of [Phantom's landing page](https://phantom.com), here are animation recommendations for Floxis:

### Animation Philosophy
**Restrained but purposeful** - Don't animate everything. Each animation should serve a function:
- Draw attention to key elements
- Provide feedback on interaction
- Create visual rhythm between sections
- Make the page feel alive without being distracting

---

### Hero Section Animations

#### Option A: Transparent Background Video
- Short looping video showing data/requests flowing through infrastructure
- Use alpha channel (transparent) video to blend with background
- Provide WebM (Chrome) + MP4 (Safari) formats
- Keep under 5 seconds, seamless loop

#### Option B: Animated Stack Diagram
- Use Lottie animation to show data flow through the stack
- Requests animate from top (Demand Partners) → through Floxis → to Publisher
- Subtle, continuous loop
- Reinforces the "infrastructure layer" positioning

#### Option C: Subtle Particle/Data Flow
- Abstract particles or dots flowing server-to-server
- Very subtle, ambient motion
- Conveys "server-side" without being literal

---

### Scroll-Triggered Animations

| Element | Animation | Trigger |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Section headings | Fade up + slight slide | Enter viewport |
| Feature cards | Staggered fade in | Enter viewport |
| Stack diagram | Progressive reveal (top to bottom) | Scroll position |
| Metrics/numbers | Count up animation | Enter viewport |
| Client logos | Fade in as group | Enter viewport |

**Implementation:** Use Intersection Observer API or libraries like:
- GSAP ScrollTrigger
- Framer Motion (React)
- AOS (Animate on Scroll)

---

### Hover Interactions

#### Feature Cards
```
Default state:  Static icon, card at rest
Hover state:
  - Card lifts slightly (translateY: -4px)
  - Subtle shadow increase
  - Icon animates (Lottie) or video plays
  - Border glow (matches brand color)
Transition:     0.2-0.3s ease
```

#### CTA Buttons
```
Default:   Solid gradient background
Hover:
  - Slight scale (1.02)
  - Glow effect behind button
  - Background shifts slightly
Transition: 0.2s ease
```

#### Stack Diagram (if interactive)
```
Hover on layer:
  - Layer highlights
  - Connected arrows pulse
  - Tooltip appears with detail
```

### Section Theme Transitions (Phantom-style)

Consider changing background color/theme as user scrolls through sections:

| Section | Background |
|---------|------------|
| Hero | Dark (primary brand) |
| Stack Diagram | Slightly lighter or gradient |
| Features | Card-based on dark |
| Metrics | Accent color background |
| Testimonial | Return to dark |
| Final CTA | Dark with gradient accent |

**Implementation:** Use scroll position to interpolate between color values, or snap to themes at section boundaries.

### Video in Feature Cards (Phantom-style)

If you have product demos or visualizations:

```
Card default:    Static thumbnail/image
Card hover:      Video autoplays (muted)
Card unhover:    Video pauses, returns to thumbnail
```

**Technical requirements:**
- Videos must be short (3-8 seconds)
- Seamless loops preferred
- Muted autoplay (no sound on hover)
- Provide poster/thumbnail for initial state
- Lazy load videos (performance)

---

### Performance Considerations

| Technique | Performance Impact | Recommendation |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| Lottie animations | Low | Use freely |
| CSS transitions | Very low | Use freely |
| Scroll-triggered fades | Low | Use moderately |
| Background video | Medium-High | One per page max |
| Hover videos | Medium | Lazy load, limit to 4-6 |
| Parallax effects | Medium | Skip (not necessary) |
| 3D transforms | Medium | Skip (doesn't fit brand) |

**Key rule:** Publishers care about page speed. Don't let animations hurt Core Web Vitals.

---

### What NOT to Animate

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Everything moving at once | Overwhelming, unprofessional |
| Aggressive parallax | Dated, causes motion sickness |
| Auto-playing video with sound | Annoying, kills trust |
| Flashy transitions | Contradicts "serious infrastructure" positioning |
| Text that bounces/shakes | Undermines credibility |
| Continuous looping distractions | Pulls focus from content |

---

### Animation Timing Guidelines

| Animation Type | Duration | Easing |
|----------------|----------|--------|
| Hover states | 0.2s | ease-out |
| Card reveals | 0.3-0.4s | ease-out |
| Section fades | 0.5-0.6s | ease |
| Number count-up | 1.5-2s | ease-out |
| Lottie icons | 1-2s loops | linear |

**Stagger delays:** When revealing multiple cards, stagger by 0.1s each.

---

### Implementation Priority

If implementing animations in phases:

**Phase 1 (Essential):**
- Hover states on buttons and cards
- Basic scroll fade-in for sections

**Phase 2 (Enhanced):**
- Lottie icons on feature cards
- Stack diagram animation
- Number count-up on metrics

**Phase 3 (Premium):**
- Hero background video/animation
- Hover-to-play videos on cards
- Section theme transitions
